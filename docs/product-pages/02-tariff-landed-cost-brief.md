## How duties actually work (the brief background)

Every import duty calculation depends on three factors:

1. **HS / HTS code** — classification determines the rate. First six
   digits universal; further digits country-specific (US HTS, EU TARIC).
   Wrong code, wrong rate.
2. **Customs value** — usually the transaction value (price paid or
   payable) plus insurance, freight, and other costs per the Incoterm.
   CIF is the most common base globally.
3. **Duty rate** — depends on HS code, country of origin, and any trade
   agreements in force. MFN, FTA, ADD, CVD, safeguard, and unilateral
   measures can apply simultaneously — or not at all.

Beyond duties, total landed cost includes VAT/GST (most countries 10–25%
on the duty-inclusive customs value), customs processing fees, the US
Harbor Maintenance Fee (0.125% on sea cargo), excise duties on alcohol,
tobacco and fuel, and inspection and compliance costs (phytosanitary
certificates, conformity assessments, lab testing).

A CT-002 brief addresses every one of these where they apply to your
specific corridor.
