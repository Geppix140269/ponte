# Features

What the product does today. Update when behaviour changes, not when code
moves.

## The commercial model

The marketplace is **free**: post, get vetted, connect. There are no listing
fees, no subscription to browse, and no commission on a deal the members close
themselves.

| Line | Price | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| Marketplace | Free | Post, vetting, anonymous matching, direct connect |
| Ponte AI | $19 per month, or $190 per year | Unlimited listing checks, AI account manager, match alerts |
| The desk, per deal | Success fee | Deal managed end to end. Percentage agreed in writing, due on closing. |
| The desk, retained | Monthly retainer | Standing sourcing or selling mandate |

This is the current model and it supersedes earlier ones. Do not reintroduce
the report shop, per report pricing, or subscription tiers for access.

## Marketplace

- Three listing kinds: an offer, a requirement, and a service.
- A stepped listing form: the product, the terms, photos and files, then a
  preview of exactly how it will appear on the board.
- Guests can build and preview a listing without an account. Sign in happens
  only at publish.
- Drafts survive the sign in trip, except photos, which must be re-attached.
- Nothing goes live until the desk approves it.
- Members stay anonymous until both sides agree to connect. Connecting is free.
- The board shows a live listing count only once there are enough listings to
  be worth stating, currently eight. Below that the heading carries no number.
  No invented numbers, no fake activity, no view counts.
- The board reads as a trading board, not as classifieds: dense rows under
  column headers, led by quantity, then incoterm, then the corridor, then the
  product and its reference, then the posted date and the poster's
  verification level. An offer and a requirement carry different rails and
  badges, because a requirement is what attracts supply.
- Photos are not on the board. They are context, not a decision, so they sit
  below the terms on the listing page and in the link preview when a listing
  is shared.
- A field nobody filled in reads "not stated". Nothing is inferred, and no
  default is shown that could be mistaken for data.
- The listing page opens with a specification table: quantity, unit, incoterm,
  origin, destination, payment terms, HS code, frequency, timing, counterparty
  role, position in the chain, posted date.
- Listings can be shared to WhatsApp with a short reference such as `PT-1234`.

## Vetting

- An AI co-pilot reviews every submission and scores it for the desk.
- A human approves or rejects. The AI does not publish anything on its own.
- Listings record who is submitting and how close they are to the goods, so a
  producer and an intermediary are not presented as the same thing.

## Ponte AI

- Free listing checks up to a limit, then $19 per month for unlimited.
- Scores and coaches a listing before it goes live.
- An account manager brief that reads the account and suggests the next move.

## On a phone, and installed

The site is a progressive web app. There is no separate mobile build and no
app store listing: the same routes, served the same way, with a phone shaped
navigation and an installable shell.

**The bottom bar** is the primary navigation under 768px: Home, Market, Post,
Verify, Account. Post is the only accented slot, because it is the one action
that puts something into the market. The bar slides away on a downward scroll
and returns on an upward one, unless the reader has asked their system for
reduced motion, in which case it stays put.

There is no duplication between the bar and the header. The header drops to the
logo, the language and a two item drawer holding Fees and Contact, the pages
someone reads once. Reading and legal pages live in the footer. A phone showing
the same five links in two places is a desktop site that has been squeezed.

**Installing.** `/manifest.webmanifest` is generated by `app/manifest.ts` and
declares a standalone display, the navy theme colour and three long press
shortcuts: board, post, verify. Android crops an installed icon to the
launcher's shape, so there is a separate padded `maskable.svg`: the
android-chrome PNGs are edge to edge and would lose the legs of the bridge to a
circular mask. The offer to install is never made on a first visit, and a
dismissal is permanent.

**Offline.** `public/sw.js` never stores a page. Not the board, not a listing,
not the account page. Online means always fresh from the network, offline means
the `/offline` page, and there is no third state where a member is reading
yesterday's board without knowing it. The only things kept are that offline
page, in each language, and assets whose filename contains a hash of their
contents and therefore cannot go stale.

That is a deliberate ceiling, not an oversight. Caching the board for offline
reading is possible, but it needs a visible "as of" timestamp on every row
first, and that is a product decision.

**Ten languages here too.** The offline page is a real route rather than a
static file, so a Spanish reader offline gets a Spanish page. The worker learns
each reader's language from the URL and stores that one. The bar uses a short
label, `nav.marketplaceShort`, because a fifth of a phone will not hold "Place
de marché".

## Languages

Ten interface languages: English, Chinese, Spanish, Arabic, French,
Portuguese, Russian, German, Hindi, Italian. Arabic renders right to left.

Listing content is translated separately from the interface, through the
`listing_translations` table, and surfaced by the "Read in" bar on a listing.
On a prefixed URL that bar opens in the reader's language when a translation
already exists. These are two different systems and should stay that way.

Terms and Privacy are English only, deliberately: a translated contract would
be a second, unreviewed instrument. Admin is English only.

## Accounts

- Sign in by magic link or Google. No passwords.
- Signing in creates the account. There is no separate registration.
- Profile roles are locked against self promotion to admin.

## The desk and advisory

- An analyst desk for scoped questions, priced by engagement.
- Introductions are papered under NCNDA before they happen.
- Ponte acts as broker and intermediary, never as principal.

## Admin

Internal, English only. Listing vetting queue, users, and the legacy order and
product screens from the retired shop, which are still present in the tree.

## Retired, do not revive

- The report shop: catalogue, categories, product pages, cart, checkout. All
  now permanent redirects.
- The Deal Sheet as a separate page. It folded into the Marketplace under a
  single door. The digest mechanism itself is kept for alerts.
- Per report pricing and any subscription tier for access to the board.
